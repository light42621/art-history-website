The Evolution of Art

An interactive journey through 24+ art periods

Prehistoric Art (c. 40,000–2,000 BCE)

Prehistoric art marks humanity’s earliest visual expression, focused on survival, fertility, and spirituality through carvings, cave paintings, and figurines.

Anonymous Lascaux Artists

These unknown creators decorated caves with depictions of animals and human symbols, expressing early storytelling and ritual practices.

Lascaux Cave Painting (c. 17,000 BCE) – Bison and horses depicted using mineral pigments.

Venus of Willendorf (c. 28,000 BCE) – Fertility figure symbolizing abundance and survival.

Ancient Egyptian Art (c. 3,000–300 BCE)

Egyptian art focused on the afterlife, gods, and divine rule, characterized by rigid forms, hieroglyphics, and symbolic proportions.

Thutmose

Thutmose was the royal sculptor credited with the iconic bust of Nefertiti, embodying Egyptian ideals of beauty and symmetry.

Bust of Nefertiti (c. 1345 BCE) – Elegance, grace, and idealized female beauty.

Mask of Tutankhamun (c. 1323 BCE) – Gold funerary mask with inlaid gemstones.

Greek and Roman Art (c. 900 BCE – 476 CE)

Classical art emphasized ideal human forms, balance, and proportion, evolving from Greek naturalism to Roman realism.

Phidias

Greek sculptor of the Parthenon's Athena and Zeus of Olympia. Master of classical harmony.

Parthenon (447–432 BCE) – Temple to Athena showcasing Doric architecture and sculpture.

Roman Portrait Bust (1st c. BCE) – Realistic depiction of leaders, showing wisdom and age.

Byzantine Art (c. 330–1453 CE)

Centered in Constantinople, Byzantine art focused on spiritual symbolism, flat gold backgrounds, and religious icons.

Anonymous Iconographers

Icons were made by monks and painters devoted to spiritual expression, not fame. The work was considered a form of worship.

Christ Pantocrator (6th c.) – Icon from Sinai showing divine authority and serenity.

Hagia Sophia Mosaics – Gold-backed religious scenes in a vast Christian basilica.

Medieval Art (c. 500–1400 CE)

Medieval art reflects religious devotion, manuscript illumination, and Gothic cathedrals, often stylized and symbolic.

The Limbourg Brothers

Flemish illuminators famous for "Les Très Riches Heures," a lavishly illustrated prayer book with detailed seasonal scenes.

Les Très Riches Heures (c. 1412) – Calendar scenes with vibrant colors and courtly life.

Chartres Cathedral (c. 1194) – Gothic architecture with stained glass masterpieces.
